The Adventure of How I Got Here

I was born in Nairobi, Kenya, where the only thing faster than a Serengeti cheetah was my curiosity about how things worked. At the age of 8, my family moved to Luanda, Angola, where I discovered that technology wasn't just a tool—it was a superhero that could save the day. I spent my childhood tinkering with gadgets, trying (and failing) to make my own version of Iron Man's suit. Spoiler: it didn't quite fly.

Fast forward to now, and my love for breaking things apart and fixing them has evolved into a passion for backend software engineering. I realized that if I could build systems that could make cities smarter or help manage resources more efficiently, I'd be on my way to becoming a real-life tech superhero (minus the cape, of course).


Goals for the Program - Becoming a Software Sorcerer

I'm joining the School of Engineering's backend software engineering program to level up my coding superpowers. I'm ready to dive into creating scalable, efficient software that could help tackle big problems—like making traffic less terrible, or maybe even building a smart city (no flying suits… yet). Ultimately, I want to be part of the next wave of tech innovators who are using code to make life better, faster, and more sustainable.
